Practice, Practice…Practice What’s the point of learning if you don’t put it into practice? Regular practice allows you to articulate complicated phrases while keeping pace and rhythm. It is critical to practice. But first, listen to the original several times (if it isn’t your own original, of course). Before you start singing, be sure you understand what you’re doing. Make a mental image of the metronome ticking. Tap with your fingers or your feet and keep track of the rhythms. And practice rushing or slowing down the song from its original tempo. Remember to sing on the beat and not to vary the tempo based on your error.
